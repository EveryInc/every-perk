# Buyer Personas — Perk Travel & Expense Platform

**Owner:** Erik Lindqvist (Growth)
**Last updated:** February 2026
**Based on:** 45 customer interviews, 2,800 customer accounts, sales team input

---

## Persona 1: "CFO Claire" — The Strategic Buyer

### Demographics
- **Title:** CFO, VP Finance, or Head of Finance
- **Company size:** 500-5,000 employees
- **Industry:** Technology, consulting, financial services
- **Age:** 40-55
- **Reports to:** CEO / Board

### Goals
- Reduce operational costs across the organization
- Gain real-time visibility into company-wide spend
- Ensure compliance with financial regulations (SOX, GDPR, local tax laws)
- Consolidate vendor stack — fewer tools, less overhead
- Impress the board with data-driven cost optimization

### Pain Points
- "I don't know what we're spending until the quarterly report."
- "Our expense policy exists in a PDF that nobody reads."
- "Reconciliation takes my team 2 weeks every quarter."
- "We have 3 different tools for travel, expenses, and cards. None of them talk to each other."
- "My auditors ask questions I can't answer quickly."

### Buying Behavior
- Evaluates tools based on ROI and total cost of ownership
- Needs to justify purchase to the board — wants clear metrics
- Cares deeply about security, compliance, and data privacy
- Long sales cycle (3-6 months), involves procurement
- Influenced by Gartner MQ, Forrester Wave, peer recommendations

### What Perk Needs to Say
- "Save X hours and $Y per year"
- "Full audit trail, SOC 2 compliant, GDPR ready"
- "Real-time spend visibility across your entire organization"
- "Replaces 3 tools with one platform"

### Objections
- "How does your pricing compare to Concur?"
- "Can you integrate with our ERP (SAP/NetSuite)?"
- "What happens to our data if we leave?"
- "Are you going to be around in 5 years?" (startup risk)

## Persona 2: "Travel Manager Tanya" — The Day-to-Day User

### Demographics
- **Title:** Travel Manager, Office Manager, EA to C-suite
- **Company size:** 200-2,000 employees
- **Industry:** Any (especially consulting, sales orgs, agencies)
- **Age:** 28-45
- **Reports to:** VP Operations, CFO, or Chief of Staff

### Goals
- Book travel for teams quickly and correctly
- Stay within budget without constant back-and-forth
- Reduce the time she personally spends on travel coordination
- Keep travelers happy (they complain when travel is hard)
- Look competent and efficient to leadership

### Pain Points
- "I spend half my Monday booking travel and chasing people for preferences."
- "Group bookings are a nightmare — spreadsheets, email chains, nobody responds."
- "I don't know if a booking is within policy until finance rejects it."
- "Travelers call me instead of using the tool because the tool is confusing."
- "When something goes wrong (cancellation, delay), I'm the one who has to fix it."

### Buying Behavior
- Doesn't make the purchase decision but has strong influence
- Evaluates tools by trying them — wants a trial/demo
- Cares most about ease of use and time savings
- Will champion internally if the product saves her visible pain
- Active in peer communities (Admin Facebook groups, EA networks)

### What Perk Needs to Say
- "Book group travel in 20 minutes, not 4 hours"
- "Your travelers can self-serve — fewer requests hitting your inbox"
- "Policy is built into the booking flow — no surprises at approval"
- "Mobile-first — your travelers can book and expense from their phone"

### Objections
- "My travelers won't use another tool — they barely use the current one"
- "Does it work internationally? We have people in 8 countries"
- "Can I customize it for our specific travel policies?"
- "What support do you offer when things go wrong?"

## Persona 3: "PM/IC Pablo" — The Frequent Traveler

### Demographics
- **Title:** Consultant, Sales Rep, Product Manager, Engineer
- **Company size:** Any
- **Industry:** Any (especially consulting, tech, sales)
- **Age:** 25-40
- **Reports to:** Director or VP

### Goals
- Submit expenses as fast as possible and move on with his life
- Understand what he's allowed to expense (so he doesn't get flagged)
- Have travel booked for him (or book it easily himself)
- Not think about expense management at all

### Pain Points
- "I hate categorizing expenses. It takes forever and I always pick the wrong one."
- "I got flagged for a policy violation but nobody told me what the policy was."
- "The receipt scanner never works. I end up typing everything manually."
- "I just want to photograph my receipt and be done. Why is this so hard?"
- "I can't tell if my expense report has been approved or is stuck somewhere."

### Buying Behavior
- Doesn't buy — uses whatever his company provides
- Will loudly complain if the tool is bad (to Travel Manager Tanya or on Slack)
- Will be delighted by anything that saves him time
- Compares to consumer apps (Uber, Venmo) — expects that level of UX

### What Perk Needs to Say
- "Snap a receipt. Done."
- "AI handles the categorization — you just confirm"
- "Know your policy before you book, not after"
- "Submit expenses in under a minute from your phone"

### Objections
- "I don't trust AI to categorize correctly — I'll still have to check"
- "The app is slower than just keeping a spreadsheet"
- "Why do I need an app for this? Just give me a credit card"

## Persona 4: "Compliance Carl" — The Risk Gatekeeper

### Demographics
- **Title:** Controller, Compliance Officer, Internal Auditor
- **Company size:** 1,000+ employees
- **Industry:** Financial services, healthcare, government contractors
- **Age:** 35-55
- **Reports to:** CFO, General Counsel

### Goals
- Ensure every expense is properly documented and categorized
- Catch policy violations before they become audit findings
- Maintain a complete, defensible audit trail
- Reduce risk of fraud, waste, and non-compliance
- Pass internal and external audits with zero findings

### Pain Points
- "I spend 40% of my time chasing people for missing receipts."
- "Our current tool doesn't tell me WHY something was flagged."
- "Approval rules don't fire reliably — I caught $10K in unapproved expenses last week."
- "I can't run a compliance report without exporting to Excel and building my own pivot tables."
- "When auditors ask questions, it takes days to pull the data."

### Buying Behavior
- Doesn't initiate purchase but has veto power
- Evaluates tools on compliance features, audit trail, and reporting
- Needs to see security documentation (SOC 2, ISO, GDPR) before approving
- Will block a purchase if compliance features are weak
- Values reliability and accuracy over speed and flashiness

### What Perk Needs to Say
- "Every AI decision has a complete audit trail"
- "Policy rules that actually enforce — not just warn"
- "Real-time compliance dashboard"
- "One-click audit reports"

### Objections
- "If AI categorizes an expense wrong, who's liable?"
- "How do I override the AI when it's incorrect?"
- "Can I see exactly why each categorization decision was made?"
- "What's your data retention policy?"
- "Do you support our industry's specific compliance requirements?"

## Persona 5: "IT Ivan" — The Technical Evaluator

### Demographics
- **Title:** IT Director, Systems Administrator, IT Procurement
- **Company size:** 500+ employees
- **Industry:** Any
- **Age:** 30-50
- **Reports to:** CTO, CIO

### Goals
- Ensure new tools integrate with existing tech stack (SSO, ERP, HRIS)
- Minimize security risk and data exposure
- Reduce vendor sprawl and integration complexity
- Keep the lights on — uptime, performance, reliability

### Pain Points
- "Every new tool is another SSO integration and another vendor security review."
- "I need API access for our internal dashboards, not just a pretty UI."
- "Our data must stay in the EU. Can you guarantee that?"
- "How does your tool handle 2,000+ concurrent users during Monday morning peaks?"

### Buying Behavior
- Technical evaluator, not the budget holder
- Will run a security assessment and pen test before approval
- Cares about API documentation, integration options, and data architecture
- Wants to talk to your engineering team, not your sales team
- Reads G2 reviews, Hacker News, Reddit

### What Perk Needs to Say
- "SAML/OIDC SSO out of the box"
- "REST API with comprehensive documentation"
- "EU data residency available"
- "99.99% uptime SLA"

### Objections
- "Do you support SCIM provisioning?"
- "What's your data processing agreement?"
- "Can we host it on-premises?"
- "What happens during an outage — do expenses still work offline?"

## How Personas Map to Purchase Journey

```
Awareness → CFO Claire (reads Gartner, sees competitor press)
            PM Pablo (complains about current tool on Slack)

Consideration → Travel Manager Tanya (researches alternatives, requests demos)
                IT Ivan (evaluates security and integration)

Decision → CFO Claire (budget approval)
           Compliance Carl (compliance sign-off)
           IT Ivan (security sign-off)

Adoption → Travel Manager Tanya (rollout, training)
           PM Pablo (daily usage)

Expansion → CFO Claire (add more teams, upgrade plan)
            Compliance Carl (deeper compliance features)
```

## Notes

- These personas are based on B2B SaaS patterns. Our largest segment is 500-2,000 employee tech companies.
- For enterprise (5,000+), add "Procurement Petra" — but she's not a product persona, she's a process gatekeeper.
- We under-index on Persona 3 (PM Pablo) in our marketing. Most of our content targets Claire and Tanya. We should create more traveler-facing content.
